Introduction¶

The TMDb movie database gathers data about movies released between 1960 and 2015.

This analysis proposes to dig through the TMDb movie database and will try to establish dependencies and independencies across the different dataset variables.

The dataset can be downloaded here.

Some Questions¶

Along the analysis, we will try to unveil certain characteristics of the dataset and address several questions, including:

  • Is there a general trend in terms of revenues, budgets and profits?
  • Which genres stand out in terms of profits?
  • Does duration (runtime) skew to certain genres and does it influence profits?
  • What are the most successful months in terms of profits?
  • Are there properties associated with profits overall?

Data Cleaning¶

As this analysis will focus on measurable outcomes, a first view of the entire dataset shows that some columns can be dropped, namely:

  • 'popularity', 'vote_count', 'vote_average', 'homepage', 'tagline', 'keywords' and 'overview' because they are biased data.
  • 'budget' and 'revenue' because it will be more accurate to use the adjusted 'budget_adj' and 'revenue_adj' columns.
  • 'release_year' because it will be more accurate to use the 'release_date' column instead.

10 columns will be dropped, 11 will remain.

Additionally, the strategy for the other columns will be as follows:

  • 'id', 'imdb_id': first, locate missing / null cells, second, count duplicates.
  • 'cast', 'genres', 'production_companies', 'director': first, locate missing cells, second, find methodology for filling na cells. As needed, split into columns using the '|' separator.
  • convert 'release_date' object type to datetime type.
  • check for how many zero values are in 'budget_adj' and 'revenue_adj' as these might weigh on some statistics.

Conclusions¶

Severe Limitations With Zero Values¶

The 'budget_adj' and 'revenue_adj' columns are overwhelmingly populated (>50%) with zero values, making quantitative analysis a biased exercise.

Possible Conclusions for Non Zero Values¶

Movies are good business: the trend in profits is updward despite plateauing revenues. However this comes at a cost with a rise in production budgets.

While movies are branded across several genres, the most successful movies are those with a taste of Action, Adventure, Drama and Comedy. Fantasy and Animation are also rising genres in recent years.

The runtime of movies is lower in recent years than it was in the early years of the dataset up to the end of the 1970s. While most movies oscillate around the 110 minutes mark, some genres keep to lower (Family, Animation) or longer runtime (War, History). Runtime does not seem to be a factor impacting profits.

The most successful movies are released during the months of May and June, followed by a second bracket for November and December.
